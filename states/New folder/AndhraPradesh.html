The Kohinoor of India

Discover the rich cultural heritage, ancient temples, and vibrant traditions of Andhra Pradesh. From the majestic Tirupati Temple to the beautiful beaches of Visakhapatnam, explore the heart of South India.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Amaravati
  • Population: 49 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar

Introduction

Andhra Pradesh, often called the "Kohinoor of India," is a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known for its ancient temples, classical dance forms like Kuchipudi, and delicious cuisine, the state offers a perfect blend of tradition and modernity. From the sacred Tirupati Temple to the historic Charminar, Andhra Pradesh is a treasure trove of cultural heritage.

Search Monuments & Places in Andhra Pradesh

Tirupati Temple

The most visited religious place in the world, dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. Known for its magnificent architecture and spiritual significance.

Charminar

A historic monument and mosque located in Hyderabad. Built in 1591, it's one of the most recognized structures of India.

Golconda Fort

A historic fort located in Hyderabad. Known for its acoustic architecture and as the center of diamond trade in medieval India.

Konark Sun Temple

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this 13th-century temple is dedicated to the Sun God and is famous for its architectural grandeur.
