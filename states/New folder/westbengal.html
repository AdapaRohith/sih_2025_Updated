West Bengal

Discover the cultural capital of India with its rich heritage, literature, art, and monuments. From the bustling streets of Kolkata to the serene hills of Darjeeling, explore the beauty of Bengal.

Explore Deep Culture

Arts & Crafts

Discover traditional Kantha embroidery, Dokra metal craft, terracotta art, handloom textiles, and Sholapith crafts

Cuisine

Savor authentic Bengali fish curry, rice, Roshogolla, Sandesh, Mishti Doi, and traditional Bengali sweets

Festivals & Traditions

Experience grand Durga Puja, Kali Puja, Poila Boishakh, and witness artistic pandals and cultural celebrations

Music & Dance

Enjoy soulful Rabindra Sangeet, mystical Baul music, classical dance, and rich literary traditions

Search Monuments & Places in West Bengal

Victoria Memorial

The Victoria Memorial is a large marble building in Kolkata, built between 1906 and 1921. It's dedicated to the memory of Queen Victoria and is now a museum.

Howrah Bridge

Howrah Bridge is a cantilever bridge over the Hooghly River in West Bengal. It's one of the busiest bridges in the world and an iconic symbol of Kolkata.

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Dakshineswar Kali Temple is a Hindu temple located in Dakshineswar near Kolkata. It's famous for its association with Ramakrishna Paramahamsa.

Darjeeling

Darjeeling is a hill station in West Bengal known for its tea industry, the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, and stunning views of Mount Kanchenjunga.

Sundarbans National Park

Sundarbans is a mangrove area in the delta formed by the confluence of the Ganges, Brahmaputra, and Meghna Rivers. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Kolkata

Kolkata is the capital of West Bengal and the cultural capital of India. It's known for its colonial architecture, literature, and vibrant street life.
