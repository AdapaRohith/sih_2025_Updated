KERALA

God's Own Country

Discover the serene backwaters, lush green landscapes, and rich cultural heritage of Kerala. From the tranquil houseboats to the beautiful hill stations, explore the paradise of South India.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Thiruvananthapuram
  • Population: 33 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar
  • Language: Malayalam

Introduction

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a state blessed with natural beauty, rich culture, and ancient traditions. Famous for its backwaters, Ayurvedic treatments, classical dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, and delicious cuisine, Kerala offers a perfect blend of nature and culture. From the tranquil backwaters to the beautiful hill stations of Munnar, Kerala is a paradise for nature lovers and culture enthusiasts.

Digital Monument: Padmanabhaswamy Temple

Explore Deep Culture

Arts & Crafts

Discover traditional Kathakali masks, coir handicrafts, wood carvings, mural paintings, and Aranmula mirrors

Cuisine

Savor authentic Kerala Sadya, fish curry, coconut-based dishes, appam, puttu, and traditional banana leaf meals

Festivals & Traditions

Celebrate Onam harvest festival, Vishu, Thrissur Pooram, and witness colorful boat races and temple festivals

Music & Dance

Experience classical Kathakali drama, graceful Mohiniyattam dance, Koodiyattam theater, and traditional music

Search Monuments & Places in Kerala

Padmanabhaswamy Temple

One of the richest temples in the world, located in Thiruvananthapuram. Known for its magnificent architecture and spiritual significance.

Kerala Backwaters (Alleppey)

A network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and inlets. Famous for houseboat cruises and serene natural beauty.

Munnar Hill Station

A beautiful hill station known for its tea plantations, scenic beauty, and pleasant climate throughout the year.

Kovalam Beach

One of the most popular beach destinations in Kerala, known for its pristine beaches and beautiful sunsets.

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary

A famous wildlife sanctuary in Thekkady, known for its elephant herds, boat safaris, and rich biodiversity.

Fort Kochi & Chinese Fishing Nets

Iconic fishing nets at Fort Kochi, a symbol of Kerala's maritime heritage and Chinese influence on the coast.
