Key Facts

  • Capital: Gangtok
  • Population: 0.6 Million
  • Best Time: Mar-May, Oct-Dec
  • Language: Nepali

Introduction

Sikkim is a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the "Land of Mystic Splendor," Sikkim is famous for its pristine landscapes, magnificent monasteries, and rich Buddhist culture. From the sacred Tsomgo Lake to the beautiful Rumtek Monastery, Sikkim offers a perfect blend of spiritual heritage, natural beauty, and cultural diversity.

Search Monuments & Places in Sikkim

Tsomgo Lake

A sacred lake surrounded by mountains, known for its spiritual significance and natural beauty.

Gurudongmar Lake

One of the highest lakes in the world at 17,800 feet, considered sacred by both Buddhists and Sikhs, with crystal-clear waters.

Buddha Park of Ravangla

A serene park featuring a magnificent 130-foot statue of Buddha, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas and peaceful meditation spaces.

Yumthang Valley

Known as the "Valley of Flowers", this scenic valley is famous for its hot springs, pristine river, and seasonal rhododendron blooms.
