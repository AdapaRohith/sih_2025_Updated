GUJARAT

The Land of Legends

Discover the rich cultural heritage, ancient temples, and vibrant traditions of Gujarat. From the sacred Somnath Temple to the beautiful Rann of Kutch, explore the heart of western India.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Gandhinagar
  • Population: 60 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar
  • Language: Gujarati

Introduction

Gujarat is a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the "Land of Legends," Gujarat is famous for its ancient temples, vibrant festivals, and diverse landscapes. From the sacred Somnath Temple to the beautiful Rann of Kutch, Gujarat offers a perfect blend of spiritual heritage, cultural diversity, and natural wonders.

Search Monuments & Places in Gujarat

Somnath Temple

One of the twelve Jyotirlinga shrines of Lord Shiva, located in Prabhas Patan near Veraval in Gujarat.

Rani ki Vav

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this stepwell is considered to be one of the finest and largest examples of stepwell architecture in Gujarat.

Gir National Park

The only natural habitat of the Asiatic lions in the world, this national park is a major wildlife conservation success story.

Great Rann of Kutch

One of the largest salt deserts in the world, famous for its white salt landscape and the annual Rann Utsav festival.
