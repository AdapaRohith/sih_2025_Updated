MAHARASHTRA

The Land of Warriors

Discover the rich heritage, magnificent forts, and vibrant culture of Maharashtra. From the bustling city of Mumbai to the ancient caves of Ajanta and Ellora, explore the heart of India.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Mumbai
  • Population: 112 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar
  • Language: Marathi

Introduction

Maharashtra is a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the "Land of Warriors," Maharashtra is famous for its magnificent forts, ancient caves, and vibrant culture. From the bustling metropolis of Mumbai to the ancient caves of Ajanta and Ellora, Maharashtra offers a perfect blend of heritage, modernity, and natural beauty.

Digital Monument: Gateway of India

Explore Deep Culture

Arts & Crafts

Discover exquisite Warli paintings, luxurious Paithani sarees, Kolhapuri chappals, and traditional handicrafts

Cuisine

Savor authentic Vada Pav, Puran Poli, Misal Pav, Bhel Puri, and traditional Maharashtrian thali

Festivals & Traditions

Experience grand Ganesh Chaturthi, Gudi Padwa, Navratri, and witness colorful processions and celebrations

Music & Dance

Enjoy vibrant Lavani dance, heroic Powada ballads, Bharud performances, and folk music traditions

Search Monuments & Places in Maharashtra

Gateway of India

An iconic monument located in Mumbai, built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.

Ajanta & Ellora Caves

UNESCO World Heritage Sites featuring ancient Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain cave monuments with magnificent rock-cut architecture and sculptures.

Mahabaleshwar

A popular hill station in the Western Ghats, known for its scenic beauty, strawberry farms, and pleasant climate throughout the year.

Shirdi

A sacred pilgrimage town and the home of the revered saint Sai Baba, attracting millions of devotees from around the world.
