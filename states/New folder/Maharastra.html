Discover the rich heritage, magnificent forts, and vibrant culture of Maharashtra. From the bustling city of Mumbai to the ancient caves of Ajanta and Ellora, explore the heart of India.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Mumbai
  • Population: 112 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar
  • Language: Marathi

Introduction

Maharashtra is a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the "Land of Warriors," Maharashtra is famous for its magnificent forts, ancient caves, and vibrant culture. From the bustling metropolis of Mumbai to the ancient caves of Ajanta and Ellora, Maharashtra offers a perfect blend of heritage, modernity, and natural beauty.

Search Monuments & Places in Maharashtra

Gateway of India

An iconic monument located in Mumbai, built to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to India in 1911.

Ajanta Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring ancient Buddhist cave monuments dating back to the 2nd century BCE.

Ellora Caves

A UNESCO World Heritage Site with 34 caves representing Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain religious traditions.

Shivneri Fort

The birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, this fort is a significant historical monument in Maharashtra.
