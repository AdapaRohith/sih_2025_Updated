KARNATAKA

One State, Many Worlds

Discover the rich heritage, culture, and monuments of Karnataka. From ancient temples to modern marvels, explore the beauty and history of this incredible state that blends tradition with innovation.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Bengaluru
  • Population: 61 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar
  • Language: Kannada

Introduction

Karnataka, known as "One State, Many Worlds," is a land of diverse landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. From the ancient ruins of Hampi to the modern IT hub of Bengaluru, from the royal heritage of Mysore to the coastal beauty of Mangalore, Karnataka offers an incredible variety of experiences. The state is home to magnificent palaces, ancient temples, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Digital Monument: Mysore Palace

Explore Deep Culture

Arts & Crafts

Discover exquisite Mysore paintings, intricate sandalwood carvings, Channapatna toys, and traditional silk weaving

Cuisine

Savor authentic Bisi Bele Bath, Mysore Pak, Ragi Mudde, Karnataka thali, and coastal seafood delicacies

Festivals & Traditions

Experience grand Mysore Dasara, Ugadi, Hampi Festival, and witness royal traditions and cultural celebrations

Music & Dance

Enjoy vibrant Yakshagana theater, classical Carnatic music, Bharatanatyam, and traditional folk performances

Search Monuments & Places in Karnataka

Mysore Palace

The magnificent Mysore Palace is a historical palace and royal residence. It's the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore.

Hampi

A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ruins of the ancient Vijayanagara Empire. Famous for its temples, monuments, and architectural marvels.

Jog Falls

One of the highest waterfalls in India, located in the Western Ghats. Known for its spectacular four-tiered cascade and natural beauty.

Gokarna

A sacred pilgrimage town known for its pristine beaches, ancient temples, and spiritual significance. Often called the 'Varanasi of the South'.

Belur & Halebidu

Ancient Hoysala architectural marvels featuring intricate stone carvings and sculptures, showcasing medieval South Indian temple art.

Coorg (Kodagu)

Known as the 'Scotland of India', famous for coffee plantations, misty hills, and beautiful landscapes in the Western Ghats.
