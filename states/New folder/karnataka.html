One State, Many Worlds

Discover the rich heritage, culture, and monuments of Karnataka. From ancient temples to modern marvels, explore the beauty and history of this incredible state that blends tradition with innovation.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Bengaluru
  • Population: 61 Million
  • Best Time: Oct-Mar
  • Language: Kannada

Introduction

Karnataka, known as "One State, Many Worlds," is a land of diverse landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture. From the ancient ruins of Hampi to the modern IT hub of Bengaluru, from the royal heritage of Mysore to the coastal beauty of Mangalore, Karnataka offers an incredible variety of experiences. The state is home to magnificent palaces, ancient temples, and UNESCO World Heritage Sites.

Search Monuments & Places in Karnataka

Mysore Palace

The magnificent Mysore Palace is a historical palace and royal residence. It's the official residence of the Wadiyar dynasty and the seat of the Kingdom of Mysore.

Hampi

A UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its ruins of the ancient Vijayanagara Empire. Famous for its temples, monuments, and architectural marvels.

Gol Gumbaz

Gol Gumbaz is the mausoleum of Mohammed Adil Shah, Sultan of Bijapur. It's famous for its massive dome and whispering gallery.

Bangalore Palace

Bangalore Palace is located in Bangalore, built in Tudor style architecture. It's a popular tourist attraction and event venue.
