MEGHALAYA

The Abode of Clouds

Discover the rich cultural heritage, magnificent landscapes, and vibrant traditions of Meghalaya. From the beautiful Cherrapunji to the unique Living Root Bridges, explore the abode of clouds.

Key Facts

  • Capital: Shillong
  • Population: 2.9 Million
  • Best Time: Mar-Jun, Sep-Nov
  • Language: Khasi, Garo

Introduction

Meghalaya is a state rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the "Abode of Clouds," Meghalaya is famous for its pristine landscapes, unique living root bridges, and rich tribal culture. From the wettest place on Earth, Cherrapunji, to the unique Mawsmai Cave, Meghalaya offers a perfect blend of natural beauty, cultural heritage, and adventure tourism.

Search Monuments & Places in Meghalaya

Mawlynnong

Known as the "Cleanest Village in Asia", famous for its pristine environment, cleanliness, and beautiful natural surroundings.

Double Decker Living Root Bridge

Unique bridges made from living tree roots, an extraordinary engineering marvel of nature created by the Khasi people.

Dawki

A border town famous for the crystal-clear Umngot River, where boats appear to float on air due to the water's transparency.

Umiam Lake

A scenic man-made reservoir near Shillong, known for its pristine waters, water sports, and beautiful surrounding hills.
